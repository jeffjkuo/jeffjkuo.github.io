About Me

Hello! I am an Economics Ph.D. candidate at The George Washington University.

I hold an M.A. degree in Economics from Maxwell School of Citizenship and Public Affairs at Syracuse University, and a joint BA/MS degree in International Business and Trade from National Chengchi University. Before heading to the U.S. for graduate study, I was a research assistant at the Institute of Economics, Academia Sinica.

My primary research interests are Trade, International Economics, and Macroeconomics. Currently, I am a Teaching Assistant of the M.A. Applied Economics Program at GW and mainly responsible for Econometrics-related classes, such as ECON6375 Applied Econometrics, ECON6376 Time Series Analysis, and ECON6379 Applied Microeconometrics.

I am also a member of the Research Program of Forecasting and the Student Association for Graduate Economists.
